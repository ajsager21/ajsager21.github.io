About me

Analyst. Athlete. Builder.

I'm a data professional with a foundation in applied econometrics, predictive modeling, and financial analysis. My work sits at the intersection of rigorous quantitative methods and clear, stakeholder-ready communication — I build the models and write the 72-page reports that explain what they mean.

After earning both my B.S. in Quantitative Economics and my M.S. in Business Analytics from UC Irvine — graduating with honors and earning a spot in Beta Gamma Sigma — I've worked across energy analytics, housing market forecasting, and financial planning. I'm particularly drawn to problems where messy, high-dimensional data hides a story that can actually change what a business does next.

Five years as a Division I track and cross-country athlete gave me a second education: in discipline, in pushing through discomfort, and in what it means to perform under pressure as part of a team. That mindset follows me into every project. In April, I'll be running the Boston Marathon — another box on the list.

When I'm not in the data, I'm out on a trail, casting a line, or hunting down a good cup of coffee.
